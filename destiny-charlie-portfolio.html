Destiny Emmanuel Charlie is a computational chemist and research scientist whose work sits at the intersection of quantum mechanical modelling, molecular simulation, and materials science. She earned her Bachelor of Science in Chemistry from the University of Calabar in May 2025 and is currently pursuing a Postgraduate Diploma in the same department.

Her undergraduate thesis — Experimental and Theoretical Assessment of the Corrosion Inhibition Efficacy of Albendazole, Secnidazole, and Diethylcarbamazine on Carbon Steel in Acid Chloride Medium — combined gasometric and gravimetric experimental methods with DFT calculations and molecular dynamics simulations to evaluate pharmaceutical compounds as environmentally friendly corrosion inhibitors.

As a Research Assistant within the Computational and Bio-Simulation Research Group (CBSRG) at UNICAL, she has co-authored over 17 peer-reviewed publications in internationally indexed journals including ACS Omega, Journal of Molecular Structure, International Journal of Hydrogen Energy, and Diamond and Related Materials. She is a member of the ACS, RSC, and the Student Chemical Society of Nigeria.
